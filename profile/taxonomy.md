# Taxonomy — Tags & Categories

Default tags and categories for wiki pages in this instance. Use these in page frontmatter
`tags:` to keep the wiki consistently browsable. Add new tags when genuinely needed, but
prefer an existing one first.

---

## Category tags (what kind of thing the page is about)

- `model` — a specific model or model family
- `agent` — agent frameworks, patterns, orchestration
- `eval` — benchmarks, evaluation methods, leaderboards
- `infra` — serving, training, hardware, cost/perf
- `tooling` — libraries, SDKs, dev tooling, IDE/agent tooling
- `safety` — alignment, safety, governance, policy
- `paper` — a research paper or preprint
- `product` — a shipped product or feature
- `org` — a company, lab, or organization
- `person` — a notable individual
- `technique` — a method or approach (e.g. RAG, distillation, RLHF)
- `dataset` — datasets and data pipelines

## Signal tags (how to treat it)

- `watch` — something to keep an eye on; feeds the "Things to Watch" digest
- `adopt` — worth adopting / already recommended
- `trial` — worth a limited experiment
- `hold` — not now; noted for context
- `deprecated` — superseded or abandoned

## Page-type tags (mirrors the index sections)

- `source` — a source summary page
- `concept` — a concept/entity page
- `analysis` — a synthesized analysis or answer

---

Notes:
- Every page should carry at least one **category tag** and one **page-type tag**.
- Apply a **signal tag** whenever a judgment has been formed (see `rubric.md`).
